DEBRIEFING

Thank you for taking part in this study, we are very grateful for your time. Here, we would like to detail the ideas behind the study, and the methods we used.

People in general update their expectations about the future more in response to good news than in response to bad news (Sharot, T. (2011). Building on these findings, we are interested in whether this bias in updating is affected (either increased or decreased) when individuals encounter information from multiple sources and/or about multiple events simultaneously. Specifically, we want to compare how individuals update their beliefs in 3 scenarios: (1) when they receive good news from two different sources/events; (2) when they receive bad news from two different sources/events; (3) when they receive conflicting information from two different sources/events (one of which presents good news and the other bad).

Please note that the statistics shown during this experiment were NOT genuine. It was necessary to manipulate these so that you were exposed to the 3 different types of scenario outlined above. You should therefore not attach credibility to any of the statistics that you saw.

We will appreciate if you don't tell other students/friends that the statistics shown in this study are not genuine, in case they participate in this experiment.
